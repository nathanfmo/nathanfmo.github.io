CSS Color Theory

Introduction

When it comes to color choices, having more options is considered better.

But with more options, comes analysis paralysis for what might be considered a small decision

(Although I'm not going to deny the power of a good color scheme in marketing or any visual display)

I find that having limited options leads to more creativity, since you have to work within boundaries and still create something spectacular.

Consider the challenge that competitors on Top Chef have in distinguishing their dish from everyone else when they're all working with the same ingredient.

Or the common exercise of writing a short story around a small prompt that writers of all skill levels will partake in to improve their writing.

And so the self-imposed limit on colors for CSS will follow two rules

  1. They must be a named color present on this list.
  2. They must be able to be reduced to three characters in their hexadecimal code, AKA they have three pairs of characters.
